The Summer (Tail)winds 

June is historically a tough month for stocks – in fact, it’s been the second worst performing month of the year. Since 1990, the S&P has logged an average loss of 0.52 percent in the month of June. You may not realize it after this week’s selloff, but the S&P is still up about 2 percent this month – and if these gains can hold, it could mean good news for the rest the year. Since 1990, there have been only six other times when the S&P gained at least 2 percent or more in the month of June. After a similar move, the momentum tends to follow through to the following month, with the S&P averaging a return of about 1 percent in July – and over the rest of the year,  the index tacks on another 5 percent, a positive trade 83 percent of the time. 

Digging into sector performance in those six instances, a few standouts emerge. The Financials sector logs an average gain of about 9 percent, trading positively 67 percent of the time. Healthcare and Industrials also outperform, gaining 8.5 percent and 7.9 percent respectively – with Healthcare the most consistent winner, trading positively 83 percent of the time.

GE’s Rough Road

On Tuesday, S&P Dow Jones Indices announced General Electric would be removed from the Dow Jones Industrial Average, replaced by Walgreens Boots Alliance. The announcement capped off a bad trading week for the stock, which lost nearly 8 percent through that announcement. Similar one-week declines historically set off a period of underperformance for the stock – a month later GE tends to lose an average of 0.21 percent in a time where the Dow and S&P 500 have logged average returns of more than half a percent. 

 

Ready To Rebound?

GE was not the only Dow component (albeit soon-to-be former), to have a rough week. Boeing and Caterpillar each logged major declines as renewed fears of a trade war with China escalated. But, history shows these declines could precede big moves higher.  

Boeing fell over 5 percent in the past week. Since 2000, a month after the aerospace giant logs similar losses, it tends to bounce back – gaining 4 percent. 

Finally, despite a modest gain on Friday, Caterpillar is still down over 6 percent in the past week. Following similar declines, the industrial giant gains more than 3 percent a month later.
